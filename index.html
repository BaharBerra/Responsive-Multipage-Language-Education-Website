Frequently Asked Questions

What does TOEFL stand for?

TOEFL stands for the Test of English as a Foreign Language. It is a standardized test designed to measure the English language proficiency of non-native speakers who intend to enroll in English-speaking universities or programs.

How long is the TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test) duration?

The TOEFL iBT has a duration of approximately 4 hours. This includes the Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing sections. Each section has a specified time limit, and there is a break between the Listening and Speaking sections.

How many sections are there in the TOEFL iBT?

The TOEFL iBT consists of four main sections: Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing. Each section assesses different language skills and is designed to provide a comprehensive evaluation of an individual's English language proficiency.

Can I use a calculator during the TOEFL exam?

No, test-takers are not allowed to use calculators in any section of the TOEFL exam. The test focuses on assessing language skills, and mathematical calculations are not a part of the evaluation.

How is the TOEFL Speaking section evaluated?

The TOEFL Speaking section is evaluated by both human raters and automated scoring systems. Raters assess aspects such as clarity, coherence, pronunciation, and vocabulary use. The integrated tasks are also evaluated based on how well test-takers integrate information from both the reading and listening passages.

What is a good TOEFL score?

A good TOEFL score varies depending on the requirements of the institution or program you are applying to. Generally, a score above 90 is considered competitive, but it's essential to check the specific score requirements of the institutions you are interested in.

How many words should I attempt in the TOEFL Independent Writing task?

While there is no strict word count requirement, it is recommended to write an essay consisting of about 300 to 350 words in the TOEFL Independent Writing task. This ensures that you adequately address the prompt and develop your ideas effectively.

How soon can I retake the TOEFL exam if I'm not satisfied with my scores?

If you are unsatisfied with your TOEFL scores, you can retake the exam as soon as 3 days after your first attempt. However, there is a limit of 21 days within any 12-month period.
